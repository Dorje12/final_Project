Introduction

Sign language is a visual language that uses hand gestures, facial expressions, and body movements to communicate. It is primarily used by deaf individuals or those with hearing impairments as a means of communication.

Importance

Sign language is crucial for the following reasons:

  • Facilitating communication and bridging the gap between deaf and hearing individuals.
  • Promoting inclusivity and equal opportunities for deaf individuals in education, employment, and everyday life.
  • Enabling deaf children to develop language skills, cognitive abilities, and social interactions.
  • Preserving and celebrating the rich cultural heritage of deaf communities.
